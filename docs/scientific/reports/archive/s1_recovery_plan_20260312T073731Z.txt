Diagnosis summary:
1. Optimization failure is not the dominant issue.
   - the completed fresh run reached `iter: 11999` with finite, evolving losses and scheduled LR decay still active in the final phase (`lr: 2e-05`); see `codex/s1_final_fresh_eval_20260312T051557Z/seqformer_eval/1/log.txt`.
   - the final refined basis is slightly better than both earlier refined tiers on `mean_best_iou` and `recall_at_0.5`, so the model did learn something:
     - historical refined `mean_best_iou=0.339383`, interim `0.342388`, final `0.344887`
     - historical refined `recall_at_0.5=0.353052`, interim `0.358699`, final `0.360850`
   - this is inconsistent with pure optimization collapse or flatlined non-learning.

2. Target conversion quality is imperfect, but it does not look catastrophic enough to explain the full failure by itself.
   - training pseudo targets are structurally similar to val GT in duration and temporal continuity:
     - train pseudo: `3067` videos, `10323` annotations, mean tracks/video `3.365830`, mean duration `21.661436`, multi-segment ratio `0.083212`
     - val GT: `837` videos, `3719` annotations, mean tracks/video `4.443250`, mean duration `21.404679`, multi-segment ratio `0.065340`
   - raw VideoCutLER on val also remains compact:
     - `2679` tracks total across `837` videos
     - max raw tracks/video `9`
     - mean raw tracks/video `3.200717`
   - because raw `C1` comes from the same pseudo-mask source family yet still outperforms refined `C2`, pure pseudo-label noise is not the best explanation for the recorded `S1 FAIL`.

3. Matcher / target construction likely contributes to the failure.
   - the Hungarian matcher uses class cost, box L1 cost, and GIoU cost, but no mask-overlap cost in matching:
     - `third_party/VNext/projects/SeqFormer/seqformer/models/matcher.py:67-90`
   - the active loss set is `['labels', 'boxes', 'masks']`; the available `loss_cardinality` exists in code but is not active:
     - `third_party/VNext/projects/SeqFormer/seqformer/seqformer.py:205-219`
     - `third_party/VNext/projects/SeqFormer/seqformer/models/deformable_detr.py:281-293`
   - in target preparation, per-frame `valid_id` is computed from `gt_ids != -1`, but only used to gate `clip_classes`; `valid` is not propagated into the matcher/loss path:
     - `third_party/VNext/projects/SeqFormer/seqformer/seqformer.py:321-337`
   - under class-agnostic single-class training, class labels do not separate one object from another, so matching is effectively box-trajectory-dominant. If pseudo boxes are noisy, duplicate or unstable assignments are plausible.

4. The dominant issue appears to be objective mismatch, specifically count/selection mismatch between training and inference that aligns directly with the `S1` fragmentation failure.
   - active inference always selects the top `10` queries per video:
     - `third_party/VNext/projects/SeqFormer/seqformer/seqformer.py:351-356`
     - `third_party/VNext/projects/SeqFormer/seqformer/seqformer.py:431-450`
   - the final refined basis therefore emitted exactly `8370 = 837 x 10` tracks, with every val video receiving exactly `10` predictions.
   - this does not reflect the training target distribution:
     - train pseudo mean targets/video: `3.365830`
     - only `3` training videos have exactly `10` targets
     - `2817` training videos have only `1-5` targets
   - the positive supervision density is very low relative to the `300` query bank:
     - mean matched positives/video `= 3.365830`
     - matched positive fraction of query slots `= 0.011219`
   - yet no active cardinality loss corrects output count, and no confidence threshold prunes low-score predictions before the fixed top-10 export.
   - the selected refined scores confirm that many emitted tracks are weak hypotheses:
     - `36.8578%` of selected tracks score below `0.2`
     - `70.4540%` score below `0.3`
     - `86.1768%` score below `0.5`
     - in `567 / 837` videos, the minimum selected score is below `0.2`
   - the failure-case video `00818` is consistent with this mechanism:
     - raw side has `5` candidate tracks and best IoU `0.948039`
     - refined side exports `10` full-length tracks, all spanning frames `0-23`
     - best refined IoU collapses to `0.014174`
   - this pattern matches the recorded gate metrics:
     - refined `fragmentation_per_gt_instance=0.892444` vs raw `0.031998`
     - refined `over_segmentation_tendency=0.323474` vs raw `0.029847`
     - refined `unmatched_gt_ratio=0.639150` vs raw `0.612261`

Dominant-issue classification:
- primary: objective mismatch
- secondary: matcher / target-construction weakness
- tertiary: supervision noise
- not supported as primary: optimization failure

- the next step should therefore be a no-training recovery diagnosis subgate, not immediate retraining.
